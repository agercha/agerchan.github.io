2016-2019


My second high school portfolio was on the theme of "intimacy". Looking back, it feels like a naive but genuine portrayal of what my adolsescent idea of intimacy was. But I also think intimacy and love are some of the only things I want to make art about (I placed all my writing in the love letters folder because I think all my writing is a love letter to something).

I think what inspired me to make a collection of artworks about intimacy was loneliness. I am so interested in loneliness, the way that it is present in the absence of intimacy and even (or especially) in the presence of intimacy. I feel like photography, to me, is particularly good at getting this sense of loneliness across, and genuine loneliness is something I would like to, one day, be able to portray in painting.

The selected pieces below come from my original portfolio on intimacy, as well as some additional works that I think provide more context, or just provoke a sense of loneliness in me.
