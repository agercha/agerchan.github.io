2016-2019

Many of these images were edited by my high school art teacher, Christine An. If you are reading this, Ms. An, thank you for all the time you spend on this and on helping me. I would not still care about art the way I do today if it was not for you.

My second high school portfolio was on the theme of "intimacy". Looking back, it feels like a naive but genuine portrayal of what my adolsescent idea of intimacy was, and I have kept thinking about it since.

I am intrigued by loneliness, the way that it is present in the absence of intimacy and even (or especially) in the presence of intimacy. I think my interest in both of these has dictated what I make art about. Many of these pieces weren't originally made for my portfolio, but I realized they spoke to how I felt about intimacy or loneliness, either when compiling the portfolio in the first place, or as I looked back at all my high school artworks post factum. I take a lot of pictures when I go out, to keep track of things I though looked interesting, and I feel like many of them speak to these emotions as well. Most times I have written, I felt heartbroken and alone, or in love, or both. Everything I write is a love letter, to something, and every painting I make is either a portrait of something or someone I love, or it is a lonely self portrait.

I felt it fitting to go back and compile my old artworks to honor when I first realized what I want to make art about.

Some of these artworks I have lost, and some I hang up in my house. One was gifted to a person who used to love it but (understandably) can't stand to look at it anymore.
